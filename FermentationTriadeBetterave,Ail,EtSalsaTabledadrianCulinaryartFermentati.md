# Fermentation Triade: Betterave, Ail, et Salsa #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Fermentation #Condiment

**Preface:** A study in controlled microbial transformation, yielding three concentrated condiments that provide depth, acidity, and umami to the plate.

**Ingredients**

- **Fermented Beetroot:**

  - 1 kg beetroot (grated)

  - 20 g sea salt

- **Roasted Beetroot:**

  - 3 medium beets (whole, skin on)

- **Pickled Beetroot:**

  - 1 beet (sliced)

  - 100 ml vinegar

  - 50 ml water

  - 50 g sugar

  - Pinch of salt

- **Seaweed Oil:**

  - 100 ml rapeseed oil

  - 5 g dried seaweed (dulse or kelp)

- **Fermented Garlic:**

  - Garlic cloves (peeled)

  - 2% fine salt (by weight)

- **Fermented Salsa:**

  - Tomatoes, chilies, onion, cilantro

  - 2% fine salt (by weight)

**Method**

1. **Fermented Beetroot:** Combine grated beetroot and sea salt. Pack tightly into a sterilized jar. Ferment at ambient temperature for 7–10 days, ensuring the mixture remains submerged.

2. **Roasted Beetroot:** Roast whole beets at 180°C until tender. Peel and reserve.

3. **Pickled Beetroot:** Combine pickling liquid ingredients and bring to a boil. Pour over sliced beetroot. Chill.

4. **Seaweed Oil:** Combine rapeseed oil and dried seaweed. Infuse gently at 60°C for 30 minutes. Strain and reserve.

5. **Fermented Garlic/Salsa:** Combine ingredients with salt. Ferment at ambient temperature for 7–14 days, monitoring acidity.

**Technical Notes:** The precise salt percentage (2%) is critical for controlling the microbial environment and ensuring a clean, acidic fermentation.

**Chef’s Intention:** These preparations provide a disciplined, concentrated expression of terroir and controlled acidity.
