# Alternatives d'Émulsifiants et de Protéines Végétales #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Molecular #VeganOption

**Preface:** A reference for natural and plant-based alternatives to standard molecular emulsifiers, ensuring stable foams and emulsions through natural protein matrices.

**Ingredients**

- **Lecithin Alternatives:** Egg yolk, sunflower lecithin, Dijon mustard, aquafaba (chickpea water).

- **Soy Protein Alternatives:** Pea protein powder, chickpea flour (besan), whey protein isolate, reduced lentil/chickpea cooking water.

**Method**

1. **Emulsification (Egg Yolk):** Introduce a minimal amount of fresh egg yolk to the liquid. Blend to create a stable emulsion (1 small yolk emulsifies 100–200 ml).

2. **Foaming (Aquafaba):** Whip chickpea cooking water to create a stable, vegan foam structure.

3. **Protein Structure (Pea/Chickpea):** Utilize pea protein powder or lightly cooked chickpea flour to add body and structure to liquids, compensating for the absence of isolated soy protein.

4. **Sourcing:** Procure alternatives through disciplined sourcing channels (pharmacists for lecithin, fitness shops for protein powders, Asian markets for soy products).

**Technical Notes:** The protein content in aquafaba and egg yolk provides the natural surfactant necessary for stable aeration and emulsification.

**Chef’s Intention:** This reference ensures the disciplined execution of texture is maintained, while offering flexibility for dietary precision.
