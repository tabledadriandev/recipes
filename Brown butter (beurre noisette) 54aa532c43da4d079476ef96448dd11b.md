# Brown butter (beurre noisette)

Applications: Sauces, glazes, dessert bases
Confidence: 92
Corrections: Lower heat, immediate chill, clarify through filter
Failure modes: Burnt solids, smoke, bitterness
Medium: Fat
Notes: Optionally re-emulsify as noisette monté.
Related Preparations: Brown butter base (https://www.notion.so/Brown-butter-base-310e36fcfbf34dec81947fbdc48c75b3?pvs=21)
Sensory cue: Hazelnut aroma, amber color
Source: EMP
Standardized: Yes
Temperature range (°C): 145–160
Time range: 5–8 min
Tools used: Saucepan, thermometer, fine strainer
Type: Reduction

### Method

1) Melt butter and cook to 145–160 °C until milk solids are amber and aroma is hazelnut. 2) Remove from heat; strain fine; chill rapidly.

### Notes

- Sensory: hazelnut, amber; no acrid smoke. Acceptance = 150–155 °C peak, zero burnt flecks after strain.
- Uses: sauces, glazes, dessert bases.
