# Velouté d'Asperge Verte et Capucine #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Sauce #Velouté

**Preface:** A vibrant, spring velouté, utilizing the delicate bitterness of nasturtium to complement the purity of green asparagus.

**Ingredients**

- **Nasturtium Green Oil:**

  - 200 g green asparagus (finely chopped)

  - 200 g nasturtium leaves

  - 150 g potatoes (peeled, chopped)

  - 80 g white onion (peeled, chopped)

  - 60 g unsalted butter

  - 300 ml vegetable stock

  - 150 ml double cream

  - 20 g garlic (peeled, chopped)

  - Fine salt (to season)

- **Velouté Finish:**

  - 6 large green asparagus (for plating)

  - 80 ml nasturtium green oil

  - 30 ml virgin olive oil

  - Nasturtium flowers, nasturtium leaves, verbena leaves and flowers (for garnish)

**Method**

1. **Nasturtium Oil:** Sauté the onion, potato, and garlic in butter until softened. Add the asparagus and vegetable stock. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.

2. Introduce the nasturtium leaves and double cream. Bring to a rapid boil, then immediately transfer to a high-speed blender. Blend until perfectly smooth.

3. Strain the resulting velouté through a fine chinois. Season with salt.

4. **Plating:** Blanch the 6 large asparagus spears. Dress with virgin olive oil. Plate the velouté warm. Finish the surface with a disciplined drizzle of the reserved nasturtium green oil and the fresh flowers and leaves.

**Technical Notes:** The rapid boiling and immediate blending of the nasturtium leaves is essential for locking in the vibrant green colour.

**Chef’s Intention:** A precise, seasonal composition that captures the ephemeral freshness of the Riviera spring.
