# Gel Fluide Sanguine et Gingembre #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Molecular #DessertComponent

**Preface:** A vibrant, acidic gel, utilizing agar powder to create a fluid, pipeable texture that delivers concentrated citrus and ginger notes.• 3.5 g agar powder

**Ingredients**

- **Base:**

  - 4 blood oranges (zest and juice)

  - 60 g ginger syrup

  - 40 g white balsamic vinegar

  - 3.5 g agar agar powder

**Method**

1. Zest the blood oranges. Juice the oranges to yield 200 g of liquid.

2. In a saucepan, combine 200 g of the juice, the zest, ginger syrup, vinegar, and agar powder. Whisk to ensure the agar is fully dispersed.

3. Bring the mixture to a rapid boil for precisely 30 seconds (to activate the agar).

4. Pour the liquid into a shallow bowl and chill immediately until completely set.

5. Transfer the set gel to a high-speed blender. Blend until the gel is completely smooth and fluid.

6. Transfer the fluid gel to a piping bottle and store chilled.

**Technical Notes:** The fluid gel technique relies on the mechanical shearing of the set agar gel to create a smooth, non-elastic, pipeable liquid.

**Chef’s Intention:** A precise, acidic component that provides a disciplined burst of flavour and a vibrant colour accent to a dessert.
