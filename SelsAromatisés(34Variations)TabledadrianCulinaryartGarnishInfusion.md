# Sels Aromatisés (34 Variations) #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Garnish #Infusion

**Preface:** A comprehensive reference for creating concentrated, aromatic finishing salts, utilizing disciplined infusion and dehydration techniques.Method: Reduce red wine to \~3–4 tbsp. Drizzle over salt on parchment paper. Air-dry or dehydrate at 50°C for 2–3 hours.

**Ingredients**

- **Base Salt:** Maldon or high-quality sea salt (200 g per batch)

- **Red Wine Salt:** 250 ml red wine (Cabernet or Syrah)

- **Black Salt:** 1 tsp activated charcoal powder

- **Lemon Zest Salt:** Fresh lemon zest

- **Other Variations:** Herbs, spices, vegetable powders (e.g., beetroot, carrot, chili)

**Method**

1. **Red Wine Salt:** Reduce the red wine to a concentrated syrup (approx. 3–4 tbsp). Drizzle the syrup over the salt on parchment paper. Air-dry or dehydrate at 50°C for 2–3 hours until completely dry.

2. **Black Salt:** Combine the Maldon salt and activated charcoal powder. Mix thoroughly until the colour is uniform. Store airtight.

3. **Zest/Herb Salts:** Combine the salt with finely zested citrus peel or finely milled herbs. Allow to air-dry or dehydrate gently to prevent moisture transfer.

4. Store all finished salts in airtight containers to maintain their brittle texture and aromatic intensity.

**Technical Notes:** The low-temperature dehydration (50°C) is essential for removing moisture without compromising the volatile aromatic compounds.

**Chef’s Intention:** These concentrated salts provide a disciplined, final seasoning and textural contrast to a finished plate.
