WHY DOES THE ICE CREAM MIXTURE NEED TO AGE?
Aging refers to the refrigeration time before churning that is necessary for developing flavor and texture. For homemade sorbets without stabilizers, aging is not needed and they can be churned immediately. However, ice creams always need some aging time, as specified in the recipes: be sure to follow the times closely.
HOW LONG WILL HOMEMADE ICE CREAM KEEP?
Homemade ice cream without stabilizers should be consumed within a week, but it will be at its best right after being churned. Most of the ice-cream recipes here include stabilizers.
