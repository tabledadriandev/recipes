5. BAKING POWDER
Baking powder is a chemical leavener composed of a mild acid (such as cream of tartar or tartaric acid); a mild alkali (usually baking soda, also known as bicarbonate of soda); and a drying element (like cornstarch or potato starch)

6. When the acid and alkali come into contact with moisture, they react to produce carbon dioxide

7. This gas produces bubbles in the batter, which make your baked goods rise

8. Double-acting baking powders in the US also react a second time when exposed to the heat of the oven

9. storage tips: Baking powder should be kept in an airtight container in a cool, dry place

10. It gradually 
loses its strength, so should be used within several months and before the use-by date
