## Overview

Because most fish and shellfish have little connective tissue, the procedures for 
cooking them are somewhat different from those for cooking meats and poultry. 
When we cook meats, we are often concerned with tenderizing the product. Fish 
is naturally tender, however, and cooking—especially overcooking—is more likely to toughen the product and dry it out.
