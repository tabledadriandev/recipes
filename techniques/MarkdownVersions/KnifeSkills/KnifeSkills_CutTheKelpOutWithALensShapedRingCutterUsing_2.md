## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. 8 to 10 g Seasoned Cream  
10 drops Rose Oil*  
Cut the kelp out with a lens -shaped ring cutter, using the same mold used for the pumpkin 
pieces, then cut the punch outs in half equatorially to half their thickness

2. Reserve the m in a 
covered container in the broth to  not dry them out; 5 pieces are needed per portion

3. Refresh the pumpkin portions with a bit more walnut oil just before plating, and season them
with salt

4. Season the sea urchin with a couple grains of salt and 2 dro ps of chicken wing garum

5. Begin by placing one tongue of sea urchin with the tip of the tongue pointing up the side of thebowl, and its flat back side facing to the left

6. Place a dot of rose fudge on the right part of thetongue and lay a slice of hazelnut on top of the fudge with the tip pointing out

7. Pipe another dotof fudge on the right side of the slice of hazelnut, then lay a piece of pumpkin onto this dot offudge

8. Next, lay the braised kelp against the pumpkin and another slice of hazelnut against thekelp

9. Then lay down your second tongue of urchin

10. Repeat this pattern around the bowl until
you have used each piece of urchin, pumpkin, hazelnut, and mushroom kelp
