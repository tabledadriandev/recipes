# Sablés au Double Chocolat #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Dessert #Baking

**Preface:** A classic _sablé_ cookie, executed with the technical precision of the Ferrandi method, yielding a rich, deeply chocolate profile.

**Ingredients**

- **Base (Yields 25):**

  - 160 g unsalted butter (softened)

  - 160 g light brown sugar

  - 50 g lightly beaten egg (approx. 1 egg, ambient temperature)

  - 1 vanilla bean (scraped)

  - 200 g all-purpose flour

  - 30 g cocoa powder (Dutch-process)

  - 1 tsp baking soda

  - ½ tsp fine salt

  - 100 g dark chocolate chips (70% cacao)

  - 100 g milk chocolate chips

**Method**

1. In a stand mixer, cream the softened butter and brown sugar until the mixture is pale and aerated.

2. Gradually incorporate the egg and vanilla bean paste.

3. Sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt. Introduce the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing only until just combined.

4. Fold in the chocolate chips with a disciplined hand.

5. Form the dough into a log, wrap, and chill for 30–40 minutes.

6. Slice the chilled dough into 25 uniform portions.

7. Bake at 175°C for 12–15 minutes until the edges are set and the center remains slightly soft.
