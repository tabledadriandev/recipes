# Pâté de Champignon et Marron, Purée de Canneberge Fermentée #TabledAdrian #CulinaryArt #Pâté #Fermentation

**Preface:** A rich, autumnal pâté, balanced by the concentrated acidity of fermented cranberry and the aromatic depth of wild herbs.

**Ingredients**

- **Mushroom Chestnut Pâté:**

  - 300 g chestnut mushrooms

  - 100 g cooked chestnuts

  - 50 ml aged brandy (optional)

  - 10 g dried porcini

  - 2 tbsp Ligurian olive oil

  - 2 garlic cloves

  - 1 shallot

  - 1 sprig rosemary

  - Tarragon sprigs

  - Fine salt & pepper

- **Fermented Cranberry Purée:**

  - 200 g fresh cranberries

  - 250 ml water

  - 1 tbsp fine salt

  - 1 tbsp sugar

**Method**

1. **Cranberry Purée:** Combine cranberries, water, salt, and sugar. Ferment at ambient temperature for 7–10 days. Blend the fermented mixture until smooth and strain.

2. **Pâté:** Sauté the shallot, garlic, and herbs in olive oil. Introduce the fresh and dried mushrooms. Cook until moisture is evaporated.

3. Deglaze with brandy (if used). Add the cooked chestnuts.

4. Transfer the mixture to a food processor. Blend until a smooth, disciplined pâté is achieved. Season precisely.

5. Chill the pâté until firm. Serve with a disciplined dot of the fermented cranberry purée.

**Technical Notes:** The fermentation of the cranberry provides a clean, lactic acidity that cuts through the richness of the pâté.

**Chef’s Intention:** A disciplined, earthy composition that balances the richness of the forest with the bright acidity of fermentation.
