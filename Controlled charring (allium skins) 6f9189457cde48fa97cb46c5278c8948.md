# Controlled charring (allium skins)

Applications: Stocks, oils, garnishes
Confidence: 80
Corrections: Peel outer layers, shorter exposure
Failure modes: Acrid bitterness
Medium: Air
Notes: Use char for infusions, not to consume whole.
Sensory cue: Even char, sweet smoke
Source: Noma
Standardized: No
Temperature range (°C): Direct flame or 250–300
Time range: 2–10 min
Tools used: Torch or grill
Type: Grill

### Method

1) Char allium skins with torch or at 250–300 °C until evenly blackened but not ashed. 2) Infuse in liquid or oil; strain.

### Notes

- Sensory: sweet smoke, no acrid bitterness. Use char for infusion, not as a whole garnish.
- Test: exposure time and distance; acceptance = clean smoke note, no harshness.
- Uses: stocks, oils, garnishes.
